Battle Types

Blind Battles hide the enemy team until the fight starts. Use your best all-around trio.

Planned Battles reveal the rival team first. Pick three Plasmoids that punish their types and cover each other.

Type Advantage

Advantage attacks deal double damage. If the defender has advantage over your attacker, your attack is resisted and deals half damage.

Hybrid Plasmoids combine two types. Both halves apply, so a hybrid can gain multiple advantages, multiple resistances, or cancel a good and bad matchup.

Power Battles

Turn on Power Battle rules before starting any Blind, Planned, Ranked, or Unranked battle.

Every attack has a 50% chance to trigger a type power. Hybrids trigger one of their two type powers when the roll succeeds.

Speed Advantage

Faster Plasmoids are more reliable attackers because they can land decisive hits before slower opponents get another turn.

In close matchups, speed can matter more than raw attack because a quick Plasmoid may finish a weakened rival first.

When To Switch

Switch when your active Plasmoid is facing a type disadvantage, is low on health, or cannot damage the opponent efficiently.

Switching usually lets the rival strike during the swap, so switch early rather than waiting until your Plasmoid is almost defeated.

Team Building

Bring three different types when possible. A balanced team should include at least one fast Plasmoid, one high-attack Plasmoid, and one sturdy Plasmoid.

Use Gacha Mode to expand your vat, then use Management Mode for your default Blind Battle team.

Gold And Rewards

Defeating a rival Plasmoid earns 1 gold. Daily Gold gives 3 free gold once per day, and Daily Missions offer three new gold objectives each day.

The UAP Dogwhistle costs 3 gold and can summon base or hybrid Plasmoids. The Twin Tone Whistle costs 6 gold and guarantees a random hybrid Plasmoid. Ranked wins raise your rank level; ranked losses lower it.

Fusion Mode

Fusion Mode unlocks when your vat has at least 6 Plasmoids.

Spend 6 gold to fuse two different non-hybrid Plasmoids into their hybrid type. The source Plasmoids are consumed, and the new hybrid gets averaged HP, attack, defense, and speed.

Risk Rules

Risk Battles require at least six Plasmoids in your vat. Defeated rivals join your vat after the battle, but defeated teammates are lost.

The attack button glows gold when your active Plasmoid has advantage and dark when the matchup is bad.
